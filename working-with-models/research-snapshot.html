Hook A standard transformer's serial compute depth ≈ its layer count; the token stream is its only recurrence. Hypothesis: per-token loss is elevated precisely on tokens that need more serial composition than the layer budget allows — and latent recurrence (looped/recurrent-depth blocks) buys lift exactly where CoT can't reach, i.e. intrinsic next-token loss on a fixed corpus where you can't emit reasoning tokens (punchlines, garden-paths, setup→payoff resolution). Distinguishes "punchlines are intrinsically surprising" from "the model lacks the depth to resolve them."
Next per-token NLL vs. recurrence depth on open recurrent-depth checkpoints (Huginn-3.5B, Ouro 1.4B/2.6B — they expose loop-count at inference), across three corpora: (a) a synthetic compositional-probability ladder with a known loss floor H(p) and a tunable serial-depth dial, (b) post-cutoff stand-up/comedy transcripts, (c) flat control prose. Prediction: added recurrence reduces loss-minus-floor more at high serial-depth than on flat prose, concentrated on punchline tokens. Ladder validates the instrument (controls token-rarity confound); comedy gives ecological validity.
