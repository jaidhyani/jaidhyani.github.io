Recurrence-loss: what would looping buy?

early scaffold

Some predictions need several steps of silent reasoning with no room to show work — punchlines, garden-path sentences. Models that can loop internally let us dial the looping up and down and see exactly where extra serial thinking pays. Pipeline built; no GPU run yet — no findings to report, and none claimed.

    Full detail (methods, numbers, caveats)

    Question. Does latent recurrence — looping computation inside a single forward pass — lower per-token prediction loss precisely where chain-of-thought can't reach: predictions that need serial composition but no visible reasoning tokens (punchlines, garden-path sentences, setup-then-payoff)? The point is to separate "punchlines are just intrinsically surprising" from "the model lacks the serial depth to resolve them in one pass." It shares the pseudo-recurrence project's thesis — a standard transformer's serial compute is capped by its layer count — and would be direct evidence for the argument that LLMs are not merely next-token predictors.
