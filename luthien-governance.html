Control Scenarios
Scenario 1
Current State
Board Control
Scott and Jai each hold 1 board vote. MGE seat is vacant. All board decisions require agreement between both founders, or Jai breaks ties via +1 share in series-specific votes.
Shareholder Control
Founders hold 100% of issued shares and 100% of voting power. No outside shareholders.
Founders control everything
Scenario 2
MGE Established
Board Control
Mission Guardian Entity receives 1 Class M share and elects a director. That director gets N+1 = 3 votes vs. founders' combined 2 votes. MGE director controls the board.
Shareholder Control
Founders still hold all economic shares and voting power. MGE holds only 1 share. Board control shifts, but shareholder control does not.
Board majority shifts to MGE
Scenario 3
Post-Investment
Board Control
Board structure unchanged unless charter is amended. Investors holding Class A shares do not elect directors. Board remains founders + MGE.
Shareholder Control
Founder Class B shares carry 7x voting vs. investor Class A shares at 1x. Founders maintain shareholder majority even with significant dilution. See voting math below.
7x voting protects founder control
Scenario 4
Founder Departs
Board Control
Remaining founder keeps their board seat. Departing founder's seat may need to be filled or eliminated. MGE director (if established) still holds N+1 votes.
Operational Control
Immediate transfer to remaining founder. Departing founder must formally request to return. Share repurchase/vesting provisions apply to departed shares.
Operational control transfers
Voting Power & Dilution Math

The 7x Multiplier Effect

Founder votes = 7,225,001 Class B shares × 7 = 50,575,007 votes
Currently authorized Class A = 1,499,997 shares × 1 = 1,499,997 votes

✓
Even if every authorized Class A share were issued today, founders would still hold 97.1% of voting power. Investors can't come close to outvoting founders under the current charter.
Dilution Breakpoint
50,575,008 Class A shares
This is the minimum number of Class A shares (at 1x voting) needed to outvote founder Class B shares (at 7x). That's ~33.7x the currently authorized 1.5M Class A shares. Authorizing more shares requires a charter amendment, which itself requires a shareholder vote — which founders control. This creates a self-reinforcing lock: founders must approve any dilution that could threaten their own voting power.

Protective Mechanisms

Layers of Mission Protection

  • PBC structure — Delaware Public Benefit Corporation. Fiduciary duties legally require balancing mission, stockholders, and stakeholders
  • 7x dual-class voting — Founder Class B shares carry 7 votes each vs. 1 vote for Class A. Founders must approve any charter changes that could dilute their control
  • Mission Guardian Entity — When established (Trust or Non-Profit, TBD), elects a director with N+1 board votes, ensuring mission-aligned board majority
  • Mission pledges — All directors and officers sign pledges to uphold the company's mission in executing their duties
  • Charter amendment lock — Increasing authorized shares or changing voting rights requires shareholder approval, which founders control via 7x voting
  • Leave of absence clause — Operational control automatically transfers to remaining founder; departing founder must formally request to return
  • Self-reinforcing design — Each mechanism protects the others. Founders can't be outvoted without their own approval. MGE provides an independent mission check even on founders.
Key insight: The governance design creates two independent checks. The 7x voting protects founders against investor override on shareholder votes. The MGE director's N+1 board votes protect the mission even against founder decisions. Together, no single party can act against the mission without approval from at least one other party.
